Work History & Achiever Pattern

Identify consistent high performance and growth trajectory

‚ñº
‚è±Ô∏è Recommended Time: 20-25 minutes

Career Progression Analysis

Role-by-Role Deep Dive: "Walk me through your most recent position. What was your title, key responsibilities, team structure, and any recognition you received?"
Repeat for last 3-4 roles, going back 5-10 years

üéØ Look for Achiever Pattern Evidence

Growth Indicators:
  • Promotions & title advancement
  • Expanding scope of responsibility
  • Increasing team/budget size
Recognition Markers:
  • Awards & formal recognition
  • Above-average raises/bonuses
  • High-visibility project assignments
üîç Red Flags to Watch For
  • Job hopping without clear advancement
  • Lateral moves with no skill/scope expansion
  • Blame-focused explanations for job changes
  • Lack of measurable achievements
4

Major Accomplishments Deep Dive

Assess impact, leadership, and problem-solving abilities

‚ñº
‚è±Ô∏è Recommended Time: 12-15 minutes per accomplishment (2-3 total)

Accomplishment Analysis Framework

Primary Question: "Tell me about your most significant accomplishment at [Company]. What project or achievement are you most proud of?"
Role-Specific Follow-up: "We're currently working on [describe relevant project]. Tell me about something comparable you've led or contributed to."

üìã Comprehensive Follow-up Questions

Context & Setup:
  • What was the business situation?
  • What were the expected results?
  • What resources did you have?
  • What was the timeline?
Execution & Impact:
  • How did you plan the project?
  • What did you personally change/improve?
  • Where did you take initiative?
  • How did you exceed expectations?
Challenges & Growth:
  • What were the biggest obstacles?
  • How did you handle conflicts?
  • What would you do differently?
  • What did you learn about yourself?

Job-Related Problem Solving

‚ñº

Real-World Challenge

Current Business Problem: "One major challenge we're currently facing is [describe specific issue]. How would you approach addressing this?"
Anchor with Experience: "What have you done that's most similar to this situation?"

üß† Problem-Solving Framework

Analysis Phase:

8

Interest Assessment & Recruiting

Gauge mutual interest and position the opportunity

‚ñº

Interest & Fit Evaluation

Interest Gauge: "Based on our conversation, how interested are you in this opportunity on a scale of 1-10? What would it take to get you to an 8 or 9?"
Career Move Assessment: "How does this role fit into your longer-term career goals? What other opportunities are you considering?"
Win-Win Positioning: "For this to be a great move for both of us, the role needs to offer stretch, growth, and impact. Let me share how this position could provide that..."
üéØ Recruiting Best Practices
  • Listen 4x more than you talk - don't oversell
  • Create appropriate urgency by mentioning other strong candidates
  • Focus on career growth opportunity, not just job duties
  • Address concerns directly and honestly
  • Get commitment on next steps and timeline
