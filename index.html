Director of Human Resources – Multi-Site Union Operations & Enterprise Talent Strategy

Middletown, IA & Milan, TN | Full-Time | American Ordnance (A Day & Zimmermann Company)

Role Overview

At American Ordnance, we’re delivering the talent foundation that powers the front lines of national security. As Director of Human Resources, you’ll lead the HR function for two large, complex unionized manufacturing facilities supporting over 1,400 employees across Iowa and Tennessee. This is a critical executive-level role with a direct line to enterprise leadership and a mandate to shape the company’s people strategy from the ground up.

From contract negotiations to AI-enabled talent transformation, you’ll lead HR operations across labor relations, performance management, talent acquisition, and workforce development. You’ll ensure compliance with government and defense regulations while building a progressive, high-impact HR function that supports organizational growth, safety, and purpose.

The Scope and Challenge

Lead Multi-Site HR Strategy in a Complex, Unionized Environment: Own and implement the HR strategy across two manufacturing sites. Within 6–9 months, deliver on key enterprise HR goals, including contract negotiations, grievance resolution, and process standardization—balancing rigor, empathy, and operational readiness.

Drive Organizational Change & Leadership Coaching: Be the transformation agent across labor and salaried teams. Coach site leaders on people development, lead DEI implementation, and roll out performance systems that drive accountability and career growth—backed by data and AI-supported insights.

Redesign Talent Systems for Growth and Compliance: Customize and operationalize enterprise-wide HR initiatives—total rewards, succession planning, and workforce analytics. Champion a proactive, compliance-forward culture while building scalable systems to retain top talent in high-risk environments.

Employee Value Proposition (EVP)

Growth: As a key member of the leadership team, you’ll influence enterprise strategy while transforming a multi-site HR function. You’ll gain exposure to national labor strategies, enterprise compliance, and executive decision-making.

Motivators: This is a rare opportunity to operate at scale, manage complexity, lead through regulatory and union challenges, and shape the future of HR in one of the country’s most mission-driven manufacturing environments.
